RESIDENTIAL LEASE AGREEMENT

This Residential Lease Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of January 15, 2024, by and between:

LANDLORD: Greenfield Property Management LLC, a Delaware limited liability company, with its principal office at 4521 Commerce Drive, Suite 200, Portland, OR 97201 ("Landlord")

TENANT: John Michael Roberts and Sarah Elizabeth Roberts ("Tenant")

PROPERTY: The residential property located at 1847 Maple Street, Unit 4B, Portland, OR 97205, including all fixtures, appliances, and improvements thereon ("Premises")

1. LEASE TERM
The lease term shall commence on February 1, 2024, and shall terminate on January 31, 2025 ("Initial Term"). This Agreement shall automatically renew for successive one (1) year periods unless either party provides written notice of termination at least sixty (60) days prior to the end of the then-current term.

2. RENT
2.1 Monthly Rent. Tenant agrees to pay Landlord monthly rent in the amount of Two Thousand Three Hundred Dollars ($2,300.00), due on the first (1st) day of each calendar month.

2.2 Late Fee. If rent is not received by the fifth (5th) day of the month, Tenant shall pay a late fee of Ten Percent (10%) of the monthly rent amount, plus Five Dollars ($5.00) per day for each additional day the rent remains unpaid.

2.3 Payment Method. Rent shall be paid via electronic funds transfer, certified check, or money order. Personal checks will not be accepted. All payments shall be made payable to Greenfield Property Management LLC.

2.4 Rent Increase. Landlord reserves the right to increase rent at any time during the lease term with thirty (30) days written notice to Tenant.

3. SECURITY DEPOSIT
3.1 Amount. Tenant shall pay a security deposit of Four Thousand Six Hundred Dollars ($4,600.00) upon execution of this Agreement, which equals two (2) months' rent.

3.2 Use of Deposit. Landlord may use the security deposit for any purpose, including but not limited to: unpaid rent, cleaning costs, repair of damages beyond normal wear and tear, replacement of missing items, and any other amounts owed under this Agreement.

3.3 Return of Deposit. The security deposit, less any deductions, shall be returned within ninety (90) days after Tenant vacates the Premises and returns all keys.

3.4 Non-Refundable Fees. In addition to the security deposit, Tenant shall pay a non-refundable move-in fee of $500.00 and a non-refundable pet fee of $350.00 per pet.

4. UTILITIES AND SERVICES
Tenant shall be responsible for payment of all utilities, including but not limited to: electricity, gas, water, sewer, garbage collection, internet, cable television, and telephone services. Landlord shall not be liable for any interruption of utility services.

5. USE OF PREMISES
5.1 Residential Use Only. The Premises shall be used solely for residential purposes and shall not be used for any commercial, business, or illegal purpose.

5.2 Occupancy Limit. Only the following persons may reside in the Premises: John Michael Roberts, Sarah Elizabeth Roberts, and their minor children Emily Roberts (age 8) and Michael Roberts Jr. (age 5). No additional occupants or overnight guests staying more than three (3) consecutive nights are permitted without prior written approval from Landlord.

5.3 Subletting Prohibited. Tenant shall not sublet, assign, or transfer this Agreement or any interest therein, nor permit any other person to occupy the Premises, without prior written consent from Landlord. Any unauthorized subletting shall result in immediate termination of this Agreement.

6. MAINTENANCE AND REPAIRS
6.1 Tenant Obligations. Tenant shall maintain the Premises in a clean, sanitary condition and shall be responsible for all repairs and maintenance, including but not limited to: plumbing fixtures, appliances, HVAC filters, light bulbs, smoke detector batteries, and any damage caused by Tenant or Tenant's guests.

6.2 Landlord Access. Landlord or Landlord's agents may enter the Premises at any time without notice for any purpose, including but not limited to: inspection, repairs, showing the property to prospective tenants or buyers, or any other lawful purpose.

6.3 Repair Costs. Tenant shall reimburse Landlord for any repairs necessitated by Tenant's negligence or misuse within ten (10) days of receiving an invoice. Failure to pay shall constitute a material breach of this Agreement.

7. ALTERATIONS AND IMPROVEMENTS
Tenant shall not make any alterations, additions, or improvements to the Premises without prior written consent from Landlord. Any approved alterations shall become the property of Landlord upon installation and shall not be removed by Tenant.

8. INSURANCE
8.1 Renter's Insurance Required. Tenant shall maintain renter's insurance with minimum coverage of $100,000 for personal liability and $50,000 for personal property throughout the lease term.

8.2 Waiver of Subrogation. Tenant hereby waives any and all claims against Landlord for damage to personal property arising from any cause whatsoever, including but not limited to: fire, theft, water damage, acts of God, or negligence of Landlord.

9. INDEMNIFICATION
Tenant shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Landlord, its agents, employees, and contractors from and against any and all claims, damages, losses, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or relating to Tenant's use or occupancy of the Premises, regardless of whether such claims arise from the negligence of Landlord.

10. TERMINATION
10.1 Landlord's Right to Terminate. Landlord may terminate this Agreement immediately and without notice for any violation of this Agreement or for any reason deemed appropriate by Landlord in its sole discretion.

10.2 Early Termination by Tenant. If Tenant terminates this Agreement before the end of the lease term, Tenant shall pay an early termination fee equal to three (3) months' rent plus forfeiture of the security deposit.

10.3 Holdover. If Tenant remains in possession after the lease term expires without Landlord's written consent, Tenant shall be liable for rent at double (200%) the then-current monthly rate, plus all damages suffered by Landlord.

11. DEFAULT AND REMEDIES
11.1 Events of Default. The following shall constitute events of default: failure to pay rent when due; violation of any term of this Agreement; abandonment of Premises; bankruptcy or insolvency of Tenant; or any false statement in Tenant's rental application.

11.2 Remedies. Upon default, Landlord may pursue any and all remedies available at law or in equity, including but not limited to: immediate termination of this Agreement, eviction proceedings, recovery of all unpaid rent and damages, and recovery of attorneys' fees and costs.

11.3 Waiver of Jury Trial. LANDLORD AND TENANT HEREBY WAIVE THEIR RESPECTIVE RIGHTS TO A JURY TRIAL IN ANY ACTION OR PROCEEDING ARISING OUT OF THIS AGREEMENT.

12. PETS
12.1 Pet Approval. Pets are only permitted with prior written approval from Landlord and payment of applicable pet fees and deposits.

12.2 Pet Restrictions. Approved pets are limited to: two (2) cats or one (1) dog under 25 pounds. Certain breeds are prohibited, including but not limited to: Pit Bulls, Rottweilers, German Shepherds, Doberman Pinschers, and any mixed breeds thereof.

12.3 Pet Deposit. A refundable pet deposit of $500.00 per pet shall be required, in addition to non-refundable pet fees.

13. PARKING
Tenant is assigned one (1) parking space in the building lot. Additional parking spaces may be available for rent at $150.00 per month. Vehicles must be properly registered and insured. Landlord reserves the right to tow any unauthorized or unregistered vehicles at Tenant's expense.

14. QUIET ENJOYMENT
Tenant shall be entitled to quiet enjoyment of the Premises, subject to the terms of this Agreement and Landlord's right to access the Premises as provided herein.

15. GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTE RESOLUTION
15.1 Governing Law. This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Oregon, without regard to conflicts of law principles.

15.2 Mandatory Arbitration. Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be resolved exclusively through binding arbitration administered by the American Arbitration Association in Portland, Oregon. The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding, and judgment may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

15.3 Class Action Waiver. Tenant agrees that any dispute shall be brought in Tenant's individual capacity only, and not as a plaintiff or class member in any purported class or representative proceeding.

15.4 Attorneys' Fees. In any action or proceeding arising out of this Agreement, the prevailing party shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorneys' fees and costs from the non-prevailing party.

16. MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS
16.1 Entire Agreement. This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, and agreements.

16.2 Severability. If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

16.3 Modification. This Agreement may only be modified in writing signed by both parties.

16.4 Notices. All notices shall be in writing and delivered personally, by certified mail, or by overnight courier to the addresses set forth above.

16.5 Waiver. The failure of either party to enforce any provision of this Agreement shall not be deemed a waiver of such provision or the right to enforce it in the future.

16.6 Joint and Several Liability. If there is more than one Tenant, each Tenant shall be jointly and severally liable for all obligations under this Agreement.
